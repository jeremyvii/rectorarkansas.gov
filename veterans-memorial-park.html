Veterans Memorial Park

“To Honor and Memorialize Those Individuals Who Have Served in the Uniform Services of the United States of America”

In the Footsteps of Heroes

Located on the east side of South Main Street just north of US Highway 49, the Veterans Memorial Park of Rector honors those who served in the Armed Forces. Developed through a partnership between the City of Rector and the Veterans' Memorial Park Association, it stands as a tribute to those who served and continue to serve.

The centerpiece is a bronze WWII soldier, sculpted by Gary Aslum, standing atop a native Arkansas boulder. This statue symbolizes all five branches of service: Army, Navy, Marines, Air Force, and Coast Guard.

Surrounding the statue are service flags, including POW/MIA, the Arkansas state flag, and the City of Rector flag—presided over by the always-illuminated United States flag.

Heritage Walkway begins at the Visitor Center and includes commemorative granite pavers engraved with the names of local heroes. Special stones honor those killed in action.

The Visitor Center, formerly the jail and water office, is now the park headquarters and home to the Rector Area Chamber of Commerce. It also features the Founders Wall, which honors major donors who helped create the park.

Land for the park was donated by Pauline Crockett, Mr. and Mrs. Sherland Hamilton, George Jernigan, and Robert Jernigan.

Support continues through the Veterans Memorial Park Association and Downtown Central/Main Street Arkansas. Visit our Facebook page.

Vietnam Veterans Memorial Day Celebration

Each year, Rector hosts a “Vietnam Veterans Welcome Home” event, including a parade and program, to honor Vietnam-era veterans who were not welcomed upon return. Veterans from multiple states attend.

Visitor Center

The Visitor Center often hosts historical events and is available for small functions like book signings. It serves as a welcoming hub at the south end of the park.
